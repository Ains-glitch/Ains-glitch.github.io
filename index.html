Projects & Investigations

Research

Spring 2025 – Present · Current

Identifying the Solar Sources of Ambient Solar Wind-Driven Substorms with the ADAPT-WSA Model

Investigating geoeffective solar wind formation linked to substorm activity using the ADAPT-WSA model. Developing methods to classify solar wind properties and identify source regions associated with geomagnetic substorms, excluding large-scale solar events.

Solar WindGeomagneticsADAPT-WSA
Fall 2025 – Present · Principal Investigator

Open-Source Solar Data for Space Weather Forecasting in Telescope Operations

Designing a real-time solar forecasting dashboard integrating SDO and NOAA open data to predict active region events and support solar telescope operations using Python Streamlit. Collaboration with the ERAU Solar Telescope Project.

Space WeatherSDOStreamlitReal-Time
Fall 2024 – Present · Principal Investigator · NASA Florida Space Grant Nominee

Bounded Transient Time Leader Emissions Tracking & Strike Prediction (BoTTLE)

Leading a NASA Florida Space Grant Consortium and ERAU-funded project predicting lightning strikes using muon detection and RADAR data. Contributions span experimental and theoretical design, literature updates, and instrumentation proposals. 2025–2026 ERAU IGNITE Grant Award Nominee.

Lightning PhysicsMuon DetectionRADARPI
Summer 2024 · NSF/NCAR Internship

Quasi-Annual Variability in the Solar 11-Year Cycle

Conducted research at the NSF National Center for Atmospheric Research on quasi-annual variability in solar bursts, identifying predictive patterns and statistical forecasting methods for space weather using MATLAB. Results presented at AGU December 2024. Mentors: Dr. Mausumi Dikpati, Kiran Jain, Breno Raphaldini.

Solar CycleNCARMATLABAGU Presentation
Spring 2025

Effects of Photometric Variability on Dynamic Habitable Zones in M-type Stars: Castor C

Investigated stellar flare activity in the M-dwarf system Castor C using data from the ERAU 1-meter telescope to assess its impact on exoplanet habitability through photometric variability analysis.

M-dwarf StarsPhotometryHabitability
Spring 2025

Photometric Structural Analysis of Galaxy NGC 3665

Performed photometric structural analysis of NGC 3665 using DECam g, r, and z filter data to model surface brightness profiles and infer evolutionary history through 1D and 2D light distribution fitting in Python.

Galaxy EvolutionDECamPython
Fall 2023 – Spring 2024 · Research Assistant

Laboratory for Solar-Magnetosphere-Ionosphere Research

Utilized IDL for advanced simulations and visualization of magnetic and electric fields, plasma density, ion and electron velocities, with specialized focus on diamagnetic cavities.

IDLPlasma PhysicsIonosphere
